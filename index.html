Abstract

The search for specific objects or motifs is essential to art history as both assists to decode the meaning of an artwork. Digitization has produced extensive art collections, but manual methods prove to be insufficient to analyze them. In the following, we present an interface and search algorithm which allows users to select image regions containing motifs or specific objects and find similar regions in an extensive image dataset, helping art historians to analyze large digitized art collections. Computer vision has presented efficient methods for visual instance retrieval in photos. However, applied to art collections, they reveal severe deficiencies because of diverse motifs and massive domain shifts induced by differences in techniques, materials, and styles. In this paper, we present a multi-style fusion approach that successfully reduces the domain gap and improves retrieval results without labeled data or curated image collections. Our region-based voting with GPU-accelerated approximate nearest-neighbor search [66] allows to find and localize even small motifs within an extensive database in seconds with high accuracy. We obtain state-of-the-art on the Brueghel dataset [66,66] and demonstrate its generalization to inhomogeneous collections with a huge amount of distractors. With our easy-to-use user interface and methodological contribution, this work manifests a significant progress towards the machine-supported analysis of visual art.
